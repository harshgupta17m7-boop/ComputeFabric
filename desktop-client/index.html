How Compute Fabric Works

1. Decentralized Compute

When you connect to the Compute Fabric network, your computer acts as a secure, sandboxed "Worker Node". You donate a portion of your GPU VRAM and CPU Cores to help process complex tasks (like LLM inferences and data processing) for users around the world.

2. Resource Allocation

You have complete sovereignty over your hardware. By adjusting the sliders on the Dashboard, you can limit exactly how much power the network can utilize. If you're gaming or working, you can dial the sliders down. The network will dynamically adapt and assign you smaller, lighter tasks.

3. The Contributor Economy (Version 2)

In the upcoming Version 2 update, the tasks you compute will be verified via Merkle Roots. When your computation is verified by other nodes, you will earn native tokens as compensation for your hardware's time and energy.
